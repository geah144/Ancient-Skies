Nisanu modern-day March/April ⓘ
The Babylonian calendar had 12 lunar months, like ours — but each one began the moment astronomers first sighted the new crescent moon low on the western horizon at sunset. This means the precise start date shifts slightly from year to year.

Way of Anu ⓘ
The 12 zodiacal constellations of the Path of the Sun, associated with the sky-god Anu. These mark the ecliptic and formed the foundation of the Babylonian zodiac.
Way of Enlil ⓘ
The 33 northern constellations belonging to Enlil, god of wind and sky. These stars never set below the horizon as seen from Babylon.
Way of Ea ⓘ
The 15 southern constellations belonging to Ea, god of wisdom and the deep waters. These stars rise and set near the southern horizon.
